About me

Short bio

Dr. Jürgen Hackl is a senior researcher at the Data Analytics Group of the University of Zurich. His research interests lie in urban resilience and span both computational modeling and network science. Much of his work has been on improving the understanding, design, and performance of complex interdependent infrastructure systems, affected by natural hazards. In the networking area, he has worked on characterizing multi-layer spatially embedded random network using concepts from spatial statistics and graph theory. Presently, he works on getting a better understanding of how the topology of the system influences dynamic processes and how this can be used to decrease the complexity of computational models. In order to transfer this knowledge to the industry, he co-founded the start-up Carmentae Infrastructure Management, helping infrastructure managers in their decision-making processes. Furthermore, he has a long history of supporting a sustainable digital world by developing and maintaining various open source libraries. Currently, he is the lead developer for the open source project pathpy
